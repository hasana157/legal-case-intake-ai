# Landlord and Tenant Act - Key Provisions

## Chapter 1: Preliminary

### Definition of Terms

**Tenant**: A person who has entered into or continues in possession of a building or land in pursuance of a contract of tenancy.

**Landlord**: A person who owns the property and leases it to the tenant.

**Building**: Any structure intended for human habitation or for use as a place of business, or for the storage of goods.

**Rent**: The consideration, whether in money or otherwise, to be paid by the tenant to the landlord.

## Chapter 2: Conditions of Tenancy

### Section 5: Notice for Termination of Tenancy

**5.1 Notice Period**: 
- For residential properties: The tenant must provide 30 days written notice to vacate
- For commercial properties: The tenant must provide 60 days written notice to vacate
- For industrial properties: The tenant must provide 90 days written notice to vacate

**5.2 Form of Notice**: 
- Notice must be in writing and clearly state the intention to vacate
- Notice must be delivered personally or sent via registered post
- The notice period starts from the date of receipt

**5.3 Effect of Non-Compliance**: 
- If a tenant fails to give proper notice, the landlord may pursue eviction proceedings
- The tenant may be liable for rent during the notice period

### Section 6: Landlord's Right to Terminate

**6.1 Valid Grounds for Termination**:
- Non-payment of rent for more than 30 days
- Breach of tenancy agreement
- Illegal use of the property
- Causing damage to the property beyond normal wear and tear

**6.2 Eviction Process**:
1. Landlord must provide written notice citing specific grounds
2. Notice must allow 60 days for the tenant to remedy the breach (if remediable)
3. If tenant fails to comply, landlord may file eviction suit
4. Court will examine the case and pass judgment

## Chapter 3: Security Deposit

### Section 10: Security Deposit Provisions

**10.1 Amount**: 
- Residential: Not exceeding 3 months' rent
- Commercial: Not exceeding 6 months' rent
- Industrial: Not exceeding 9 months' rent

**10.2 Interest**: 
- Security deposits earning interest below the bank rate shall bear simple interest at the bank rate

**10.3 Refund of Deposit**:
- Upon termination of tenancy, landlord must refund deposit within 30 days
- Landlord may adjust legitimate claims (unpaid rent, damages) from the deposit
- Any withheld amount must be documented and explained in writing

**10.4 Non-Refundable Component**: 
- Processing fee (if any) must be clearly stated in tenancy agreement
- Non-refundable fee cannot exceed one month's rent

## Chapter 4: Tenant's Rights and Obligations

### Section 12: Tenant's Rights

**12.1 Quiet Enjoyment**: 
- Tenant has the right to peacefully enjoy the property
- Landlord cannot enter without 24-48 hours notice (except emergencies)
- Landlord cannot disturb the tenant's use of common areas

**12.2 Maintenance Responsibility**: 
- Landlord is responsible for structural repairs and major systems
- Landlord must maintain common areas
- Landlord must provide water, electricity, and sanitation facilities

**12.3 Rent Increase**: 
- Rent cannot be increased more than once per year
- Increase should be reasonable (typically 5-10% in residential)
- Notice of increase must be given 60 days in advance

### Section 13: Tenant's Obligations

**13.1 Payment of Rent**: 
- Tenant must pay rent on the agreed date
- Rent should be paid to the landlord or authorized agent
- Late payment may attract interest (as per agreement)

**13.2 Maintenance of Property**: 
- Tenant is responsible for minor repairs and maintenance
- Tenant must not cause damage beyond normal wear and tear
- Tenant must keep the property clean and hygienic

**13.3 Prohibited Activities**: 
- No subletting without landlord's written permission
- No commercial activity in residential property (unless agreed)
- No alteration to the structure without permission

## Chapter 5: Disputes and Remedies

### Section 20: Legal Remedies for Breach

**20.1 Tenant's Remedies for Landlord's Breach**:
- Right to withhold rent if essential services are not maintained
- Right to claim damages for breach of quiet enjoyment
- Right to terminate lease if landlord fails to maintain property

**20.2 Landlord's Remedies for Tenant's Breach**:
- Right to recover unpaid rent with interest
- Right to file for eviction
- Right to claim damages for unauthorized alterations
- Right to recover deposits withheld for legitimate reasons

### Section 21: Dispute Resolution

**21.1 Procedure**:
1. First attempt: Direct negotiation between landlord and tenant
2. Second attempt: Mediation through local authority
3. Final: Court proceedings before District Court

**21.2 Court's Authority**:
- Court can award damages for breach
- Court can grant specific performance
- Court can order eviction with proper notice period
