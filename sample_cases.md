# Sample Case Narratives for Testing

Use these sample narratives to test the Case Intake application. Copy and paste them into the application to see how it handles different case types.

## Sample 1: Property Damage - Negligence (Civil)

**Jurisdiction**: District Court  
**Case Type**: Civil

```
Mr. Rajesh Kumar's neighbor, Mr. Vikram Singh, owns a shop adjacent to Kumar's residential house. 
On January 15, 2023, due to faulty electrical wiring in Singh's shop, a fire broke out. The fire 
spread rapidly to Kumar's house, causing severe damage. The estimated loss to Kumar's property is 
approximately ₹50,000, including furniture, electrical items, and structural damage to the walls.

Kumar immediately requested Singh to compensate for the damages, but Singh has consistently refused 
to acknowledge his responsibility. After waiting for two months without any positive response, Kumar 
consulted with his lawyer, Advocate Priya Sharma, a senior lawyer at Singh & Associates law firm.

On March 1, 2023, Advocate Sharma filed a case for negligence and recovery of damages against Vikram Singh 
in the District Court of Mumbai. The case has been registered as Case No. 2023/D-1234.

Evidence available:
1. Photographs of the damaged property taken immediately after the incident
2. Official report from the Mumbai Fire Department confirming the fire originated from Singh's shop
3. Written statements from three neighbors who witnessed the fire (Mrs. Neelam Kapoor, Mr. Ashok Verma, Ms. Ananya Gupta)
4. Medical report for minor injuries sustained by Kumar's family
5. Insurance assessment report valuing the damage
6. Email correspondence with Singh refusing to cooperate

Kumar is seeking complete compensation of ₹50,000 for the property damage, plus an additional ₹10,000 
for emotional distress and medical expenses.
```

## Sample 2: Contract Breach - Commercial (Business)

**Jurisdiction**: Commercial Court  
**Case Type**: Corporate/Commercial

```
ABC Manufacturing Limited, a Delhi-based company, entered into a supply agreement with XYZ Electronics 
Private Limited on December 1, 2022. Under the contract, XYZ Electronics agreed to supply 1,000 units 
of industrial grade circuits at ₹500 per unit, totaling ₹5,00,000, with delivery scheduled for January 31, 2023.

ABC Manufacturing made full payment of ₹5,00,000 on December 15, 2022. However, XYZ Electronics failed 
to deliver the goods by the agreed date. Despite multiple reminders and written requests, the company 
failed to provide any shipment till February 28, 2023.

Due to the non-delivery, ABC Manufacturing lost a critical contract with its primary client, 
Rajesh Industries, resulting in a loss of ₹2,00,000 in anticipated revenue. Additionally, ABC Manufacturing 
had to procure circuits from an alternate supplier at a premium price of ₹550 per unit, resulting in 
an additional loss of ₹50,000.

ABC Manufacturing's Director, Ms. Neha Kapoor, consulted with Advocate Rohan Mehta specializing in 
commercial law. The case was filed on March 10, 2023 in the Commercial Court of Delhi.

Key Evidence:
1. Signed contract with XYZ Electronics (dated December 1, 2022)
2. Payment receipt and bank transfer documentation
3. Email correspondence showing reminders sent on January 15, February 5, and February 20
4. Email response from XYZ stating they cannot fulfill the order
5. Lost contract with Rajesh Industries (email confirmation)
6. Alternative supplier invoice at ₹550 per unit
7. Proof of payment to alternate supplier
8. Internal emails discussing the financial impact

ABC Manufacturing is seeking:
- Recovery of the paid amount of ₹5,00,000
- Compensation for lost revenue of ₹2,00,000
- Compensation for premium costs of ₹50,000
- Interest at the rate of 18% per annum from the date of default
```

## Sample 3: Family Matter - Maintenance (Family Court)

**Jurisdiction**: Family Court  
**Case Type**: Family

```
Ms. Anjali Desai filed for maintenance and child support against her estranged husband, Mr. Arjun Desai, 
in the Family Court of Mumbai. The couple was married on June 15, 2015, at the Mumbai Registrar's office.

They have two children:
1. Aditya (boy, age 8, born March 3, 2016)
2. Pari (girl, age 5, born July 10, 2019)

Ms. Desai worked as a school teacher until April 2022 when she stopped working to care for the children 
after Arjun asked her to quit. Arjun, a software engineer working at TechCorp India, earns approximately 
₹1,50,000 per month. Ms. Desai currently has no income.

The couple separated on January 15, 2023, after irreconcilable differences. Arjun has not provided any 
financial support since the separation. Ms. Desai is struggling to meet basic needs including rent, food, 
education fees, and medical care for the children.

On April 20, 2023, Ms. Desai filed a case for:
1. Monthly maintenance for herself (₹30,000 per month)
2. Child support for both children (₹20,000 per month total)
3. Custody of both children
4. Educational expenses for Aditya (school fees: ₹15,000 per month)

Ms. Desai is represented by Advocate Priya Malhotra from the Women's Law Center.

Evidence:
1. Marriage certificate (Registrar's office, Mumbai)
2. Birth certificates of both children
3. Arjun's employment letter showing salary
4. Bank statements showing salary deposits (₹1,50,000 monthly)
5. Proof of child educational enrollment
6. Medical reports of children
7. Rent payment receipts
8. Emails showing Arjun's refusal to provide support
9. School fee receipts and invoices

The case was filed on April 20, 2023, in Family Court, Mumbai, Case No. FC/2023/5678.
```

## Sample 4: Employee Rights - Labour (Labour Court)

**Jurisdiction**: Labour Court  
**Case Type**: Employment

```
Mr. Suresh Patel worked as a Senior Manager (Sales) at GlobalTech Solutions Pvt Ltd for 8 years and 
3 months, from January 15, 2015, to April 15, 2023. His last drawn salary was ₹1,20,000 per month.

On April 15, 2023, without any prior warning or notice, Suresh was terminated from his position. 
No performance review or formal warning was issued before the termination. He was told verbally that 
his services were no longer required due to "organizational restructuring."

Suresh was entitled to:
1. Three months' notice as per his employment contract
2. Full and final settlement
3. Gratuity (8 years 3 months of service)
4. Earned leave (45 days unused)
5. Performance bonus for the current financial year

However, GlobalTech Solutions only paid:
- One month's salary in lieu of notice (₹1,20,000)
- Gratuity of ₹2,40,000 (calculated at ₹29,000 per year)
- No payment for earned leave or bonus

The company claimed they paid everything due, but their calculation was incorrect. According to Suresh's 
employment contract and labor laws, the correct amounts should be:
- Three months' notice: ₹3,60,000
- Gratuity: ₹3,00,000
- Earned leave: ₹45,000 (45 days at ₹1,000 per day)
- Performance bonus: ₹1,20,000

Suresh has filed a case in Labour Court demanding the rightful dues. His case was filed on May 20, 2023.

Represented by: Advocate Vikram Singh (Labour Law Specialist)

Evidence:
1. Employment contract (January 15, 2015)
2. Latest salary slip (April 2023)
3. Full and Final Settlement statement from company
4. Email termination notice
5. Bank statement showing payments received (₹3,60,000)
6. Email records showing bonus practices in previous years
7. Leave record showing 45 days unutilized
8. Copy of the appointment letter mentioning notice period

Relief sought:
- Outstanding three months' notice pay: ₹1,20,000
- Correct gratuity difference: ₹60,000
- Earned leave compensation: ₹45,000
- Performance bonus: ₹1,20,000
- Total: ₹3,45,000 with interest at 18% per annum
```

## Sample 5: Consumer Complaint - Product Defect

**Jurisdiction**: Consumer Court  
**Case Type**: Consumer

```
Mr. Harish Choudhury purchased a high-end refrigerator (Model XYZ-500) from ElectroMart India Ltd 
on August 10, 2022, for ₹85,000. The product had a 5-year manufacturer warranty.

After just 4 months of use, on December 5, 2022, the refrigerator developed a cooling problem. 
The temperature inside would not go below 10°C despite the thermostat being set to the coldest setting. 
All food items were spoiling within 2-3 days.

Harish immediately contacted ElectroMart's customer service center. The company sent a service technician 
on December 10, 2022, who identified that the compressor was faulty and needed replacement. However, 
the technician quoted ₹25,000 for the replacement, claiming it was beyond warranty coverage.

Harish argued that the compressor failure was a manufacturing defect and should be covered under the 
5-year warranty. However, ElectroMart refused to honor the warranty claim. Harish then purchased food 
items separately from outside during the repair period, incurring additional expenses of ₹5,000.

After consulting with a lawyer, Harish filed a complaint in the District Consumer Protection Authority 
on January 15, 2023, against:
1. ElectroMart India Ltd (seller)
2. XYZ Electronics Manufacturing Co. (manufacturer)

Harish's claims:
- The refrigerator is defective due to manufacturing fault
- The product is unfit for its intended purpose
- The company is unfairly refusing warranty coverage
- Harish suffered financial losses and inconvenience

Relief sought:
- Replacement of the refrigerator with a new model (or full refund of ₹85,000)
- Reimbursement for extra food expenses: ₹5,000
- Compensation for mental harassment and inconvenience: ₹10,000
- Cost of the complaint proceedings: ₹1,000

Represented by: Advocate Meera Sharma (Consumer Rights)

Evidence:
1. Purchase receipt (August 10, 2022)
2. Warranty card
3. Service call record (December 10, 2022)
4. Service technician's report
5. Technician's quotation (₹25,000)
6. Bills for separately purchased food items
7. WhatsApp/Email correspondence with customer service
8. Letter refusing warranty claim
9. Email to customer service complaining about non-cooperation
```

## How to Use These Samples

1. Copy the narrative text (the paragraph under the header)
2. Paste it into the "Enter the case narrative" text area
3. Ensure the Jurisdiction and Case Type match the sample
4. Click "Extract Case Info"
5. Review the AI-extracted information
6. Edit any fields that need correction
7. Export as JSON when satisfied

## Tips for Testing

- Try each sample to see how the AI handles different case types
- Intentionally miss some information (dates, names) to see how the AI handles incomplete data
- Try combining parts of different narratives to create complex cases
- Modify the narratives slightly to see how the AI adapts
